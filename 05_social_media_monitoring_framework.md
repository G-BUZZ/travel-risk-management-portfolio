# 05. Social Media and Public-Signal Monitoring Framework

## Purpose

Public-signal monitoring can support travel risk management by identifying disruptions, public events, safety alerts, and early indicators of instability. It must be done lawfully, proportionately, and with privacy safeguards.

## Scope

This framework only uses:

- public official accounts;
- verified institutional channels;
- public transport and airport notices;
- public event announcements;
- mainstream media posts;
- public crisis alerts.

## Out of scope

The following are excluded:

- private profiles;
- doxxing;
- scraping protected data;
- bypassing access controls;
- profiling individuals;
- collecting sensitive personal data;
- making accusations based on unverified posts.

## Monitoring categories

| Category | Examples | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Transport disruption | Airport closures, strikes, road blocks | Update briefing and transfer plan. |
| Civil unrest | Planned demonstrations, crowd movement, police advisories | Map relevance to hotel/venue route. |
| Weather / natural hazard | Flooding, extreme heat, storms | Check flight, health, and transport impact. |
| Public security alert | Official warning or incident notice | Escalate depending on location and specificity. |
| Rumour / unverified claim | Viral posts without confirmation | Log as unverified; seek corroboration. |

## Signal triage

| Level | Meaning | Example response |
|---|---|---|
| Green | No direct relevance | Log only if useful. |
| Yellow | Contextual relevance | Monitor and seek corroboration. |
| Orange | Likely travel impact | Update travel note and notify reviewer. |
| Red | Direct or urgent safety relevance | Immediate escalation to responsible security function. |

## Example monitoring note

> A verified city transport account reports planned road closures near the central district from 18:00 to 22:00. The planned airport-to-hotel route passes through the district. Recommendation: use alternate route and avoid late arrival window.

## Privacy principle

Collect the minimum information needed to support a travel security decision. Avoid storing names, handles, photos, or personal identifiers unless there is a clear legal basis and organisational procedure.
