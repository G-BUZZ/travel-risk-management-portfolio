# 09. Limitations, Ethics and Privacy

## Core principles

This project follows five principles:

1. **Legality** - use lawful public sources only.
2. **Proportionality** - collect only what is needed for travel risk support.
3. **Privacy by design** - avoid unnecessary personal data.
4. **Traceability** - document sources, dates, assumptions, and limitations.
5. **Non-alarmist reporting** - communicate risk clearly without exaggeration.

## Limits of the methodology

- Synthetic data cannot prove real-world travel risk competence.
- Public sources may be outdated, incomplete, biased, or geographically broad.
- Social media signals can be noisy and misleading.
- A source reliability score is not a guarantee of accuracy.
- Accommodation scoring is only as good as the criteria and source quality.
- The model does not replace professional travel security systems.

## Privacy safeguards

The project excludes:

- private individual monitoring;
- sensitive personal data;
- collection of handles or profile identifiers;
- photos or biometrics;
- unauthorised scraping;
- profiling based on protected characteristics.

## Security safeguards

The project avoids:

- tactical advice to evade authorities or security controls;
- operational details about law enforcement procedures;
- identification of real targets, vulnerable facilities, or individual travellers;
- claims of real intelligence capability.

## How to describe this project honestly

Use wording such as:

> A portfolio project demonstrating structured travel risk documentation and public-source research methods using synthetic data.

Avoid wording such as:

> Operational intelligence assessment, law enforcement investigation, threat hunting against real targets, or professional security advice.
