# 06. Urgent Communications Triage

## Purpose

Travel security teams may receive urgent messages, emails, posts, calls, or internal reports.

The goal is to separate routine messages from items that require review or escalation.

This file does not provide a threat assessment model. It only shows a structured junior-level triage approach for organising information and escalating appropriately.

## Triage factors

| Factor | Question |
|---|---|
| Specificity | Does the message mention a specific place, time, person, event, or route? |
| Imminence | Is there a near-term timeframe? |
| Credibility | Is the source known, corroborated, or official? |
| Relevance | Does it relate to current travellers, venues, hotels, or planned routes? |
| Severity | Could it affect physical safety, movement, access, or emergency response? |
| Pattern | Is it isolated or part of repeated reporting? |

## Triage levels

| Level | Label | Meaning | Action |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Routine | Administrative or low relevance. | Log or redirect. |
| 2 | Watchlist | Potential relevance but unclear. | Monitor and seek corroboration. |
| 3 | Urgent review | Relevant, specific, or time-sensitive. | Notify responsible security reviewer. |
| 4 | Immediate escalation | Credible and potentially serious. | Escalate according to organisational procedure. |

## Example triage decisions

| Message type | Level | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Generic complaint about airport delays | 1 | Low security relevance unless recurring. |
| Multiple reports of roadblocks near hotel district | 3 | Current route impact and time sensitivity. |
| Official police alert near meeting venue | 4 | Official source and direct relevance. |
| Anonymous vague online rumour | 2 | Monitor only; requires corroboration. |

## Analyst discipline

The analyst should not dramatise, investigate private individuals, or make unsupported claims.

The task is to:

- structure the information;
- preserve context;
- separate facts from assumptions;
- seek corroboration;
- escalate according to internal procedure.
