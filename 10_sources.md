# 10. Sources and Reference Categories

This project is methodological and uses synthetic data. The following public sources and source categories informed the structure of the workflow.

## Official and institutional source categories

- National foreign ministry travel advice pages.
- EU consular protection guidance for EU citizens abroad.
- Airport and transport authority notices.
- Local municipal and police public alerts.
- Public health authorities and international health organisations.
- Reputable media and specialist security context sources, where corroborated.

## Examples of public sources to consult in real travel-risk work

| Source category | Example use |
|---|---|
| Foreign ministry travel advice | Baseline country risk, entry requirements, local law, safety/security. |
| EU consular protection pages | Consular support principles for EU citizens outside the EU. |
| FCDO / other national travel advice | Cross-checking safety, security, health, and legal differences. |
| U.S. State Department Travel Advisories | Risk indicators and advisory level comparison. |
| OSAC public/security reports where accessible | Security context and travel advisories. |
| Airport / transport authority pages | Real-time operational disruptions. |
| WHO / ECDC / national health sources | Public health and disease outbreak context. |

## Source-use rule

Do not copy a source into a brief without explaining:

- what claim it supports;
- when it was checked;
- whether it is official, secondary, or contextual;
- what its limitations are;
- whether it needs corroboration.

## Disclaimer

This file is a source framework, not a real-time source list. Always check current official sources before real travel.
